Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 7, 2025

Eaude Furniture ("we," "our," or "us") is deeply committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, engage with our services, or interact with us in any other way. We understand the importance of transparency and aim to provide you with a clear understanding of our data practices. By accessing or using our website and services, you agree to the terms outlined in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with these terms, please do not use our website or services.

This policy is designed to comply with applicable data protection laws and regulations, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for our European customers, and other relevant privacy frameworks globally. We regularly review and update our privacy practices to ensure they align with evolving legal requirements and best industry standards. Your trust is paramount to us, and we are dedicated to maintaining the confidentiality and integrity of your personal data.

1. Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our services, fulfill your orders, and enhance your overall experience with Eaude Furniture. This information can be broadly categorized into information you provide to us, information we collect automatically, and information we obtain from third parties.

1.1. Information You Provide to Us

When you interact with our website or services, you may voluntarily provide us with personal information. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Contact Information: Your name, email address, postal address, shipping address, and telephone number. This is essential for processing orders, delivering products, and communicating with you regarding your purchases or inquiries.
  • Account Information: If you create an account with us, we collect your username, password, and any other information you choose to add to your profile, such as preferred delivery times or special instructions.
  • Payment Information: When you make a purchase, we collect payment details such as credit card numbers, billing addresses, and other financial information. Please note that we use secure, PCI-DSS compliant third-party payment processors, and we do not store your full credit card details on our servers.
  • Demographic Information: Information such as your age, gender, preferences, and interests, which helps us understand our customer base better and tailor our offerings. This information is often provided voluntarily or inferred from your interactions.
  • Communications and Interactions: Records of your communications with us, including emails, chat transcripts, customer service calls, and feedback forms. This helps us improve our customer support and resolve any issues you may encounter.
  • Customization Details: If you utilize our bespoke services, we collect detailed information about your design preferences, material choices, dimensions, and any specific requirements for your custom furniture pieces. This may include photos or sketches you provide.
  • Survey and Promotion Information: Data you provide when participating in surveys, contests, promotions, or signing up for newsletters and marketing communications. This helps us understand your preferences and send you relevant updates.
  • User-Generated Content: Any content you submit to our website, such as product reviews, testimonials, comments, or photos. Please be mindful that any information you post publicly may be visible to others.

1.2. Information We Collect Automatically

As you navigate and interact with our website, we may automatically collect certain information about your equipment, browsing actions, and patterns. This data helps us analyze website performance, improve user experience, and detect potential issues.

  • Device Information: Information about the device you use to access our website, including your IP address, operating system, browser type, device identifiers, and mobile network information.
  • Usage Data: Details of your visits to our website, including traffic data, location data, logs, and other communication data, as well as the resources that you access and use on the website. This includes pages viewed, time spent on pages, clickstream data, and referring URLs.
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to collect information about your browsing behavior. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us recognize you, remember your preferences, and track your activities. For more detailed information, please refer to our Cookie Policy.
  • Location Information: We may collect approximate location information derived from your IP address. With your consent, we may also collect precise location data from your mobile device if you enable location services.

1.3. Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties, which helps us to enhance our services and provide a more personalized experience. These sources may include:

  • Payment Processors: While we do not store full credit card details, our payment processors may share limited transaction information with us (e.g., transaction ID, payment status) to confirm successful payments.
  • Analytics Providers: We receive aggregated and anonymized data from analytics providers (e.g., Google Analytics) about website usage and performance.
  • Marketing and Advertising Partners: We may receive information from partners who assist us with marketing campaigns, such as demographic data or interests, to help us target our advertising more effectively.
  • Social Media Platforms: If you interact with us on social media platforms (e.g., liking our page, commenting on posts), we may collect information about these interactions in accordance with the platform's privacy policy.
  • Publicly Available Sources: We may collect information from publicly accessible databases or sources to verify details or enhance our customer profiles.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes, primarily to operate our business, provide our services, and communicate with you. Our use of your data is always aligned with the purposes for which it was collected and in compliance with applicable laws.

2.1. To Provide and Manage Our Services

  • Order Fulfillment: To process your orders, manage payments, deliver products, and handle returns or exchanges. This includes coordinating with logistics partners and ensuring timely delivery.
  • Account Management: To create and maintain your user account, allowing you to access your order history, manage your preferences, and streamline your shopping experience.
  • Customer Support: To respond to your inquiries, provide technical support, resolve issues, and offer assistance related to our products and services.
  • Bespoke Services: To facilitate custom design consultations, manage your customization requests, and ensure the accurate production of your bespoke furniture.
  • Website Functionality: To ensure the proper functioning of our website, personalize your experience, and provide features like wishlists, shopping carts, and product recommendations.

2.2. For Communication and Marketing

  • Transactional Communications: To send you essential updates regarding your orders, account status, shipping notifications, and policy changes.
  • Marketing Communications: With your consent, to send you newsletters, promotional offers, information about new products, sales, and events that may be of interest to you. You can opt-out of these communications at any time.
  • Personalized Marketing: To tailor marketing messages and advertisements based on your browsing history, purchase behavior, and preferences, ensuring that the content you receive is relevant to you.
  • Surveys and Feedback: To invite you to participate in surveys, provide feedback, or review products, which helps us improve our offerings and services.

2.3. For Business Operations and Improvement

  • Analytics and Research: To analyze website usage, track trends, measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns, and understand customer behavior. This helps us identify areas for improvement and optimize our website and product offerings.
  • Product Development: To inform the development of new products and services, based on customer preferences, market trends, and feedback.
  • Security and Fraud Prevention: To protect our website, systems, and customers from fraud, unauthorized access, cyberattacks, and other malicious activities. This includes verifying identities and monitoring for suspicious behavior.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal processes, and governmental requests. This includes responding to subpoenas, court orders, and other legal obligations.
  • Internal Reporting: For internal business reporting, accounting, auditing, and other administrative purposes.

2.4. For Personalization

  • Content Customization: To deliver content, product recommendations, and advertisements that are tailored to your interests and preferences based on your past interactions and browsing patterns.
  • User Experience Enhancement: To optimize the layout, navigation, and overall user interface of our website to make it more intuitive and enjoyable for you.

3. How We Share Your Information

We may share your personal information with third parties in certain circumstances, always ensuring that such sharing is necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy and that appropriate safeguards are in place. We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

3.1. Service Providers

We engage trusted third-party service providers to perform functions on our behalf and help us operate our business. These providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the purposes for which we disclose it to them. Categories of service providers include:

  • Payment Processors: To securely process your transactions (e.g., Stripe, PayPal).
  • Shipping and Logistics Partners: To deliver your furniture products (e.g., courier services, freight forwarders).
  • IT and Infrastructure Providers: For hosting our website, data storage, and IT support.
  • Analytics Providers: To help us understand website usage and performance (e.g., Google Analytics).
  • Marketing and Advertising Agencies: To assist with our marketing campaigns, ad targeting, and email communications.
  • Customer Support Platforms: To manage customer inquiries and communications.
  • Professional Advisors: Such as legal, accounting, or consulting firms.

3.2. Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale of all or a portion of our assets, your personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you via email and/or a prominent notice on our website of any such change in ownership or control of your personal information.

3.3. Legal Requirements and Protection of Rights

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in the good faith belief that such action is necessary to:

  • Comply with a legal obligation or valid governmental request (e.g., subpoena, court order).
  • Protect and defend the rights or property of Eaude Furniture, our customers, or the public.
  • Prevent or investigate possible wrongdoing in connection with the services.
  • Protect the personal safety of users of the services or the public.
  • Protect against legal liability.

3.4. With Your Consent

We may share your information with third parties when we have your explicit consent to do so. For example, if you agree to participate in a co-branded promotion or allow us to share your testimonials publicly.

3.5. Aggregated or Anonymized Data

We may share aggregated or anonymized data that cannot reasonably be used to identify you. This type of data is often used for research, statistical analysis, and business intelligence, helping us to understand broad trends without compromising individual privacy.

4. Data Security

The security of your personal information is a top priority for us. We implement a variety of technical, administrative, and physical safeguards designed to protect your data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. While no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure, we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information.

  • Encryption: We use Secure Socket Layer (SSL) technology to encrypt data during transmission between your browser and our servers, particularly for sensitive information like payment details.
  • Access Controls: Access to your personal data is restricted to authorized employees, contractors, and service providers who need the information to perform their job functions. All personnel are trained on data privacy and security protocols.
  • Data Minimization: We only collect and retain personal data that is necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy, minimizing the risk associated with data breaches.
  • Regular Audits and Assessments: We regularly review our security practices and conduct vulnerability assessments and penetration testing to identify and address potential weaknesses in our systems.
  • Incident Response Plan: We have an incident response plan in place to address any potential data breaches swiftly and effectively, including notification procedures as required by law.
  • Physical Security: Our data centers and physical infrastructure are protected with robust security measures to prevent unauthorized physical access.
  • Third-Party Security: We carefully vet our third-party service providers to ensure they maintain adequate security measures for any personal data they process on our behalf.

Despite our best efforts, no security system is impenetrable. In the unlikely event of a data breach, we will take immediate steps to mitigate the impact and notify affected individuals and regulatory authorities as required by applicable law.

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. The retention period for different types of data varies depending on the purpose of collection and legal obligations.

  • Account Data: We retain your account information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you with services. If you close your account, we may retain certain data for a limited period to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce our agreements.
  • Transaction Data: Information related to your purchases (e.g., order history, payment records) is retained for longer periods as required by tax, accounting, and consumer protection laws.
  • Communication Data: Records of your communications with us may be retained for a period to allow us to refer back to past interactions for customer service improvement or dispute resolution.
  • Marketing Preferences: We retain your marketing preferences for as long as you wish to receive marketing communications from us. If you opt-out, we will retain a record of your preference to ensure we do not send you unwanted communications.
  • Anonymized Data: Data that has been anonymized or aggregated in such a way that it can no longer identify you may be retained indefinitely for analytical and research purposes.

When your personal information is no longer required, we will securely delete or anonymize it in accordance with our data retention policies and applicable laws.

6. Your Data Protection Rights

Depending on your location and applicable data protection laws, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information. Eaude Furniture is committed to facilitating the exercise of these rights.

6.1. General Rights

  • Right to Access: You have the right to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): In certain circumstances, you have the right to request the deletion of your personal information. This right is not absolute and may be subject to legal exceptions (e.g., for legal compliance or ongoing transactions).
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information in certain situations (e.g., if you contest the accuracy of the data).
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller, where technically feasible.
  • Right to Object to Processing: You have the right to object to the processing of your personal information for direct marketing purposes or when the processing is based on our legitimate interests (unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for the processing that override your interests).
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on your consent to process your personal information, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

6.2. Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the contact details provided in Section 10 of this Privacy Policy. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request to ensure the security of your data. We will respond to your request within the timeframes required by applicable law.

Please note that some requests may be subject to limitations or exceptions under applicable law. For example, we may not be able to delete information that we are legally required to retain for tax or regulatory purposes, or information necessary for the fulfillment of an ongoing contract.

7. International Data Transfers

Eaude Furniture operates globally, and your personal information may be stored and processed in countries outside of your country of residence, including India where our primary operations are based. These countries may have data protection laws that are different from those in your jurisdiction.

When we transfer your personal information across international borders, we take appropriate measures to ensure that your data remains protected in accordance with this Privacy Policy and applicable data protection laws. These measures may include:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): Implementing standard contractual clauses approved by relevant regulatory bodies (e.g., the European Commission) for transfers of personal data to third countries.
  • Adequacy Decisions: Relying on adequacy decisions by the European Commission or other relevant authorities, which recognize that a country provides an adequate level of data protection.
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs): For internal transfers within a corporate group, if applicable.
  • Consent: Obtaining your explicit consent for specific transfers, where appropriate.

By using our website and services, you understand and agree that your personal information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your country of residence. We are committed to ensuring that any such transfers are conducted in a manner that safeguards your privacy rights.

8. Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 18 without parental consent, we will take immediate steps to delete such information from our records.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately using the details in Section 10. We encourage parents and legal guardians to monitor their children's internet usage and to help enforce our Privacy Policy by instructing their children never to provide personal information through our services without their permission.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or business operations. When we make changes, we will revise the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we are protecting your information.

If we make material changes to this Privacy Policy that significantly affect how we use or share your personal information, we will notify you by posting a prominent notice on our website or by sending you an email, as required by law. Your continued use of our website or services after the effective date of the revised Privacy Policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated terms.
