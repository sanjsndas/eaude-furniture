Cookie Policy

Last Updated: July 7, 2025

Welcome to Eaude Furniture's comprehensive Cookie Policy. This document explains how Eaude Furniture ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website, eaudefurniture.com, and the choices you have regarding their use. We are committed to transparency and providing you with clear information about how your data is collected and utilized to enhance your browsing experience, personalize our services, and ensure the security of our platform. By continuing to use our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy, unless you have adjusted your browser settings to disable them. We encourage you to read this policy in conjunction with our Privacy Policy for a complete understanding of our data practices.

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information and to remember your preferences. Cookies enable us to recognize your device and store certain information about your past actions or preferences. This allows us to offer a more tailored and efficient browsing experience, remembering items in your shopping cart, login details, and site preferences. Our use of cookies is designed to optimize functionality and provide relevant content, ensuring that your interaction with Eaude Furniture is as seamless and enjoyable as possible.

Understanding Our Cookie Practices: 40 Key Aspects of Data Control and User Interaction

This section provides an in-depth look at various aspects of our cookie usage, your data control, and how you can interact with us regarding these practices. Each point offers detailed explanations to ensure a thorough understanding of our commitment to your privacy.

1. What are Cookies and Why Do We Use Them?

Cookies are small pieces of data, typically text files, stored on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone) by your web browser when you visit a website. They serve various purposes, primarily to make websites function more effectively and to provide information to the website owners. At Eaude Furniture, we use cookies to enhance your browsing experience by remembering your preferences, such as language settings or items in your shopping cart. They also help us analyze website traffic, understand user behavior, and personalize content and advertisements. Without cookies, certain functionalities of our website might not work correctly, and your experience could be less efficient. Our use of cookies is always aimed at improving the service we provide to you.

2. First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies Explained

Cookies can be categorized based on who sets them. First-party cookies are set by the website you are visiting (i.e., Eaude Furniture). They are typically used for essential site functionalities like remembering your login status or shopping cart contents. Third-party cookies, on the other hand, are set by domains other than the one you are visiting. These are often used for advertising, analytics, or social media integration. For example, a third-party analytics cookie might track your browsing behavior across multiple sites to provide us with aggregated data about user trends. We carefully select our third-party partners to ensure they adhere to strict data protection standards, safeguarding your privacy even when external cookies are in use.

3. Session Cookies vs. Persistent Cookies

Cookies also differ in their duration. Session cookies are temporary and are automatically deleted from your device when you close your web browser. They are crucial for maintaining your session as you navigate through different pages of our website, ensuring, for instance, that your shopping cart remains intact as you browse products. Persistent cookies, however, remain on your device for a set period or until you manually delete them. These cookies are used to remember your preferences or actions across multiple sessions, such as remembering your login details for future visits or recalling your preferred language setting. We use a combination of both to optimize both immediate functionality and long-term user experience.

4. Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential Website Functionality

These cookies are absolutely essential for the website to function correctly and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. Without these cookies, services you have asked for, like accessing secure areas of the website or using the shopping cart, cannot be provided. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information and are crucial for the basic operation of our e-commerce platform. Your consent is not typically required for these cookies as they are fundamental to the service you are requesting.

5. Performance Cookies: Enhancing Site Speed and Efficiency

Performance cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, for instance, which pages visitors go to most often, and if they get error messages from web pages. These cookies do not collect information that identifies a visitor. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. It is only used to improve how a website works. For example, they help us understand which parts of our website are most popular, allowing us to optimize page loading times and streamline navigation. By analyzing this data, we can continuously refine our website's performance, ensuring a faster and more responsive experience for all our users.

6. Functionality Cookies: Remembering Your Preferences

Functionality cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your user name, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personal features. For instance, if you customize certain elements of the website, these cookies can remember those settings for your next visit. They can also be used to remember changes you have made to text size, fonts, and other parts of web pages that you can customize. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites. They are designed to make your experience more convenient and tailored to your individual preferences.

7. Targeting/Advertising Cookies: Delivering Relevant Content

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement as well as help measure the effectiveness of the advertising campaign. They are usually placed by advertising networks with the website operator’s permission. They remember that you have visited a website and this information is shared with other organizations such as advertisers. Quite often, targeting or advertising cookies will be linked to site functionality provided by the other organization. We use these cookies to present you with Eaude Furniture products and promotions that are most likely to appeal to your design preferences, based on your browsing history on our site and other relevant platforms.

8. Analytics Cookies: Understanding User Behavior

Analytics cookies, often provided by third-party services like Google Analytics, help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. They track metrics such as the number of visitors, the pages they visit, the time spent on each page, and the path they take through the site. This data is crucial for us to identify popular content, pinpoint areas for improvement, and optimize our website's structure and content to better serve our users. The insights gained from these cookies are aggregated and do not personally identify you, but they are vital for our continuous website development and user experience enhancement efforts.

9. Social Media Cookies: Seamless Sharing and Interaction

These cookies are set by a range of social media services that we have added to the site to enable you to share our content with your friends and networks. They are capable of tracking your browser across other sites and building up a profile of your interests. This may impact the content and messages you see on other websites you visit. If you do not allow these cookies, you may not be able to use or see these sharing tools. For example, if you click a "Like" button on our product page, the social media platform might set a cookie to remember your preference and potentially show you related content on their platform. We use these to enhance our brand visibility and allow for easy content sharing.

10. Managing Your Cookie Preferences Through Our Banner

Upon your first visit to our website, you will encounter a cookie consent banner. This banner provides you with the option to accept all cookies, decline non-essential cookies, or customize your preferences. This tool is designed to empower you with control over your data. If you choose to decline, only strictly necessary cookies will be placed on your device. If you accept, all types of cookies will be utilized to provide a full-featured experience. Customization options allow you to select which categories of cookies (e.g., performance, advertising) you wish to enable or disable. Your choice will be remembered for future visits, though you can always change it later.

11. Changing Your Cookie Preferences in Your Browser Settings

Beyond our on-site banner, most web browsers allow you to manage your cookie preferences through their settings. You can typically configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is set. You can also delete existing cookies from your browser history. The exact steps vary depending on your browser (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Please note that blocking all cookies, especially strictly necessary ones, may significantly impact the functionality and performance of our website and many other websites you visit. We recommend consulting your browser's help documentation for detailed instructions on managing cookie settings.

12. The Role of IP Addresses in Data Collection

An Internet Protocol (IP) address is a numerical label assigned to each device connected to a computer network that uses the Internet Protocol for communication. While not a cookie, your IP address is often collected alongside cookie data. We use IP addresses to analyze trends, administer the site, track users’ movement, and gather broad demographic information for aggregate use. IP addresses are also crucial for security purposes, helping us detect and prevent fraudulent activities or unauthorized access to our systems. In some cases, your IP address may provide approximate geographical location information, which helps us tailor content or services relevant to your region, without identifying your precise location.

13. Web Beacons and Pixel Tags: Invisible Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies like web beacons (also known as pixel tags or clear GIFs). These are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user's computer hard drive, web beacons are embedded invisibly on web pages or in emails. They help us understand whether emails have been opened, which links are clicked, and the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. This allows us to optimize our email communications and website content based on user engagement, providing a more relevant experience without storing extensive data on your device.

14. Data Security Measures for Cookie-Related Information

The security of data collected through cookies and other tracking technologies is paramount. We implement robust technical and organizational measures to protect this information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. This includes using encryption for data in transit, maintaining secure servers, and implementing strict access controls. While no online data transmission can be guaranteed to be 100% secure, we continuously review and update our security protocols to safeguard your information. Our commitment extends to ensuring that any third-party partners who process cookie data on our behalf also adhere to high security standards, protecting your privacy throughout the data lifecycle.

15. Data Retention Policies for Cookie-Derived Data

The duration for which we retain data collected via cookies varies depending on the cookie type and its purpose. Session cookies are deleted as soon as you close your browser. Persistent cookies have varying expiration dates, typically ranging from a few hours to several years, depending on their function (e.g., remembering login details versus long-term analytics). Aggregated or anonymized data derived from cookies, which cannot be used to identify you personally, may be retained for longer periods for statistical analysis and business intelligence. We adhere to strict data retention policies, ensuring that data is only kept for as long as necessary to fulfill its intended purpose and comply with legal obligations, after which it is securely deleted or anonymized.

This right is not absolute and may be subject to legal exceptions, such as when we are required to retain data for legal compliance, tax purposes, or to fulfill ongoing contractual obligations. If you wish to exercise your "right to be forgotten," please contact our privacy team. 19. Your Right to Object to Processing of Cookie Data

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data, including that collected via cookies, where the processing is based on our legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. For direct marketing, you have an absolute right to object, and we will cease processing your data for such purposes immediately upon receiving your objection. To exercise this right, please contact us through the provided channels.

20. Your Right to Data Portability for Cookie-Related Information

The right to data portability allows you to obtain and reuse your personal data for your own purposes across different services. This means you can request to receive your personal information, including certain data collected through cookies that is processed by automated means based on your consent or for the performance of a contract, in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. Furthermore, you have the right to transmit that data to another controller without hindrance from us, where technically feasible. This empowers you to have greater control over your data and facilitates its transfer between different service providers, enhancing interoperability and user choice.

36. Browser Fingerprinting vs. Cookies

While cookies are the primary method for tracking, it's important to differentiate them from browser fingerprinting. Browser fingerprinting is a more advanced, cookie-less tracking technique that collects information about your web browser's configuration (e.g., plugins, fonts, screen resolution) to create a unique "fingerprint" of your device. This fingerprint can then be used to identify you across different websites. Eaude Furniture primarily relies on cookies for tracking and personalization as outlined in this policy. While we may use standard analytics tools that collect some browser configuration data, our core tracking mechanisms are cookie-based, providing you with clearer control and transparency over your data.
